Hospital infections

Ronald Campbell
CHCF Center for Health Reporting
at USC Annenberg

Beginning in October 2014, the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services began fining hospitals for certain hospital-acquired infections. Hospitals were graded on a scale from 1 (best) to 10 (worst). The worst 25 percent, those with a score of 7 or higher, were docked 1 percent of their Medicare reimbursement for the federal 2014-2015 fiscal year.

I've collected information about the fines here. If you have questions, email me at rwcampbe@usc.edu, and please share stories with me on Twitter @campbellronaldw

CMS Data

Each file below is a text file. With the simple of Readme, each consists of columns separated by a tab can be opened in any spreadsheet, database, statistical or mapping program.

  • Readme
  • Hospital reference: Names, addresses and more for 4,700 hospitals, updated in December 2014.
  • HAC raw scores: For the data journalists among you. You'll need to know how to join it with the hospital reference and, possibly, other data.
  • HAC hospital basic scores: Raw scores combined with the hospital names and addresses. Filter to find your state and county.
  • HAC hospital detailed scores: The nitty gritty - not just the basic scores but how badly hospitals did on each component.
  • Map of fined hospitals: Nothing fancy - just a big map showing how widespread the problem is - some 800 hospitals with HAC scores of 7 or higher.
